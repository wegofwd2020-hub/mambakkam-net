## Who I Am

My name is Sivakumar Mambakkam. Most people call me Siva.

I am a husband, father, technologist, architect, mentor, and lifelong student of
systems. Professionally, I have spent more than 36 years helping organizations
build technology capabilities that outlast their projects across
financial services, healthcare, automotive, aerospace, retail, and software
product industries.

Personally, I remain fascinated by learning. I enjoy exploring the intersections
between technology, leadership, systems thinking, history, philosophy, and human
progress.

## Where I'm From

My family is native to Mambakkam. We were farmers there, and stayed farmers until a
couple of generations back, when the family migrated to Madras and moved into
engineering and other business ventures.

## What I Believe

> Technology changes rapidly. Human challenges change slowly.

The same holds for AI. The model is abundant and the raw content a commodity;
the scarce, valuable work is _shaping_ it — setting the scope and the boundaries
so it becomes an outcome you can trust.

People still seek understanding, purpose, clarity, capability, and progress. The
best technology helps people achieve those goals.

Organizations rarely struggle because of a lack of technology. More often they
struggle because complexity obscures understanding. When people understand
clearly, progress becomes possible.

## Why These Names

Sanskrit or Tamil — the choice follows the topic. Whichever language fits what the thing
actually does is the one that gets to name it.

Pramana is प्रमाण — proof, a valid means of knowledge. It imparts the knowledge an
organization has to get right, governance and compliance, and then produces the evidence
that it happened. The name is literal.

Thittam is திட்டம், Tamil for plan. It runs productions — film, construction, software,
live events — from one codebase. The name carries the intent precisely.

Kathai Chithiram is கதை சித்திரம், picture from story. A therapist or a parent gives it a
simple story; it returns an animated one the child can understand. Accessibility of
software systems is a topic I keep returning to, and here I wanted the tools now available
in the AI world to help people with different special needs take part in society.

## What I'm Learning Now

Right now I am working through agentic AI — systems that plan and act on their own — and
where that fits in content generation for very different audiences: children with special
needs, students across grades, professionals working through governance and compliance.

The systems I have built so far are deliberately not that. They are pipelines I control
end to end, because when content reaches a child or an auditor, provenance matters more
than autonomy. The question I keep turning over is when handing control to the agent is
worth it — and how you would govern an agent as rigorously as I govern a pipeline today.
