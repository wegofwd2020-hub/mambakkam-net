Ayyanar worship is one of the oldest and most widespread forms of village religion in Tamil Nadu.
As the guardian of the village boundary, he is worshipped to ward off evil, protect crops,
and ensure the safe passage of travellers. The deity is traditionally housed in open-air shrines
at the edge of the village, flanked by large clay horse figures — offerings from devotees fulfilled.
