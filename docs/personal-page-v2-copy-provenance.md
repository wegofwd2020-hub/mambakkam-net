## §1 — "Why These Names" (shipped PR #68)

Placement: after "What I'm Building", before "What I'm Learning Now".
§3 ("Why Kathai Chithiram Exists") is folded in as the closing paragraph.

Siva's source notes:

- Name choice (Sanskrit or Tamil) follows the appropriateness of the topic.
- Kathai Chithiram = "picture from story": a therapist or parent gives a
  simple story, the software returns an animated one the child can understand.
- Pramana = a valid means of knowledge; imparts knowledge on topics like
  governance and compliance.
- Accessibility is a recurring focus — use the tools now available in AI to
  help people with different special needs take part in society.

Shipped copy (live):

> Sanskrit or Tamil — the choice follows the topic. Whichever language fits
> what the thing actually does is the one that gets to name it.
>
> Pramana is प्रमाण — proof, a valid means of knowledge. It imparts the
> knowledge an organization has to get right, governance and compliance, and
> then produces the evidence that it happened. The name is literal.
>
> Thittam is திட்டம், Tamil for plan. It runs productions — film,
> construction, software, live events — from one codebase. The name carries
> the intent precisely.
>
> Kathai Chithiram is கதை சித்திரம், picture from story. A therapist or a
> parent gives it a simple story; it returns an animated one the child can
> understand. Accessibility of software systems is a topic I keep returning
> to, and here I wanted the tools now available in the AI world to help
> people with different special needs take part in society.

Shipped copy (live):

> Right now I am working through agentic AI — systems that plan and act on
> their own — and where it fits in generating content for very different
> audiences: children with special needs, students across grades, and
> professionals working through governance and compliance.
>
> The systems I have built so far are deliberately not agentic. They are
> pipelines I control end to end, because when content reaches a child or an
> auditor, provenance matters more than autonomy. What I keep turning over:
