# /people/siva-m — §4 generation memory lines: provenance

Sanitized record of the three generation memory lines added to the "Four
Generations of Computing" timeline on `/people/siva-m`. Each line is the
`highlight` field on its generation in the `generations` array of
`src/data/people/siva-m.mdx`, rendered in italic by `Timeline.astro`.
Shipped live in PR #72; confirmed on <https://mambakkam.net/people/siva-m>.

For each generation: Siva's own source notes, then the line that shipped.
The LinkedIn-derived candidate anchors from the interview worksheet, and the
fabricated illustrations used only to model rhythm, have been removed — every
published word is Siva's own. Generation Four's line predates this work and
is unchanged.

---

## Generation One (1989–1999) · Building Systems

Siva's source notes:

> As a programmer, teacher and technical writer, my views were enriched by
> the different user views — end user, product owner, business manager. Each
> had a very different language and everyone was speaking English. That was
> my education into how people from different walks of life perceive how they
> would benefit from computers.

The nuance he sharpened in review: everyone spoke English, but each used
different words for the same thing; the real lesson was listening past the
words to understand the problem.

Shipped line (live):

> _End users, product owners, business managers — all speaking English, each
> with different words for the same thing. Learning to listen past the words
> to the actual problem was the education._

---

## Generation Two (2000–2010) · Connecting Systems

Siva's source notes:

> I was responsible to migrate three products from a vendor, along with their
> source code, to our facility. I learned to manage migration, teaching our
> development team to take ownership of the source code and get ready to
> support it and add new features. All hands on, extremely intense,
> time-bound.

Shipped line (live):

> _I moved three products and their source out of a vendor's hands into ours
> — intense, time-bound, teaching a team to own code they hadn't written a
> line of._

---

## Generation Three (2011–2020) · Scaling Systems

Siva's source notes:

> With my personal experience with a Raspberry Pi, I built a POC on how
> vehicle access points (doors, liftgate) could be remotely opened from the
> cloud. This helped me understand how to work with the different electronic
> systems in existing vehicles.

Shipped line (live):

> _A Raspberry Pi I'd wired up at home became the proof a car's doors and
> liftgate could open from the cloud — my way into the electronics already
> living inside the vehicle._
